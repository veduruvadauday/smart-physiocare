Lumbar Spondylosis



Lumbar Spondylosis


Lumbar spondylosis is a type of lower back arthritis that most typically affects adults over 60. Physiotherapy can help alleviate the symptoms of lumbar spondylosis.

Causes for Lumbar Spondylosis:

The lumbar spine consists of five vertebrae separated by discs that act as shock absorbers and make the spine more flexible. As humans age, the joints in the lumbar spine wear and tear, resulting in additional bone growths called osteophytes surrounding the vertebrae and discs to try to repair the damage.



osteophytes can stiffen the spine and sometimes impinge on the surrounding nerves, causing additional complications.


Symptoms of Lumbar Spondylosis:

  • • Stiffness
  • • Low back pain

Neurological Symptoms:

  • • Tingling/Pins and Needles
  • • Abnormal Reflexes
  • • Gait Problems
  • • Co-ordinations Problems

What should I do if I've been diagnosed with lumbar spondylosis?

If you believe you may be experiencing lumbar spondylosis symptoms, you should visit your doctor at once. They will be able to provide you with an accurate diagnosis of your problem and may recommend that you get an X-ray or an MRI scan if your symptoms are more severe. It is critical that you begin a physiotherapy program with SMART Physio Care as soon as you have been diagnosed with lumbar spondylosis by a professional.


Physiotherapy for Lumbar Spondylosis:

Your physical therapy regimen will include lumbar spine strengthening and range-of-motion exercises to aid in its mobility. TENS and Wax therapy will both be employed as pain management techniques.Pacing guidance is crucial because it enables you to maintain control of your health and prevents it from taking over your life.


Other Procedures Include:

  • • Hydrotherapy
  • • Balance Exercises
  • • Ergonomics Assessment (Biomechanical)
  • • Postural Realignment

Typically moderate and treatable with conservative measures, lumbar spondylosis is treatable without surgery. However, if symptoms are ignored and become more severe, surgery may be a possibility, and recovery time may be greatly extended.
